Website Evaluations

Bad Website: Berkshire Hathaway

Berkshire Hathaway is a holdings conglomerate based out of Omaha, Nebraska. Here's where it falls short:

  • Contrast: While there isn't any insane contrasting issues, some text is so small that it almost blends into the white background.
  • Repetition: Every link looks the same, as if half of this site was carbon-copied to the other half. Symmetric, but bad.
  • Alignment: Everything is centered (minus the bullet points) until you enter the footer, where it is all left aligned.
  • Proximity: There is so much white space that everything feels far apart.

Overall, I would like to know how much this conglomerate paid someone to make this website. It's that... interesting.

Good Website: Best Buy

Best Buy is an online/physical department store with all of your technological needs. Here's whats good about it:

  • Contrast: Very good contrasting. You can clearly read text and everything is straight-forward
  • Repetition: The main page of Best Buy is super non-repetitive, with all boxes of your favorite items popping up to catch your interest.
  • Alignment: Content is aligned and stays within equal margins.
  • Proximity: Clickable items have ample whitespace between them for reading and clicking, with little room for error.

Overall, very uniquely designed website. Best Buy has always been a good website to interact with and is fun for the eyes.
