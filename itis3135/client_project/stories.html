When to Use Your VOICE

Using your voice means standing up for yourself or someone else when something doesn't feel right. It can be hard — but your voice has power, and your boundaries matter.

Speak Up When...

  • You feel pressured to do something you're uncomfortable with
  • You notice a friend withdrawing or acting differently
  • Someone makes a joke or comment that feels harmful or invasive
  • You hear rumors or witness harassment
  • Your boundaries are not being respected, physically or emotionally

Using Your VOICE Might Look Like...

  • “I don't feel okay with that.”
  • “I noticed you haven't been yourself lately. Want to talk?”
  • Reporting a concern anonymously using our form
  • Interrupting or redirecting an uncomfortable situation
